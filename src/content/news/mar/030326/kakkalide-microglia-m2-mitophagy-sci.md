title: "Kakkalide Promotes Spinal Cord Repair by Shifting Immune Cells from Attack to Repair Mode via Mitophagy"
pubDate: 2026-03-03
brief: "A natural plant compound helps heal the spinal cord by cleaning up the 'power plants' inside immune cells, which stops them from causing harmful swelling and turns them into repair workers."
deepDive: "After SCI, resident immune cells called **microglia** become 'angry' (M1 state) and flood the injury site with inflammatory signals, causing a damaging second wave that destroys healthy nerve tissue. The goal is to flip these cells to **M2 mode** — where they act as paramedics, cleaning up debris and releasing healing signals. **Kakkalide**, a natural isoflavone compound, achieves this switch via **mitophagy** — the cell's internal trash-disposal system for broken mitochondria. After injury, damaged mitochondria leak toxic reactive oxygen species that keep microglia locked in attack mode. Kakkalide accelerates mitophagy, hauling away these broken power plants before they can poison the cell's behaviour. With the internal toxins cleared, microglia shift to M2 and begin promoting tissue repair. In animal models, Kakkalide treatment produced **reduced neuroinflammation, greater nerve cell survival, and improved motor recovery**."
