title: "Biomimetic Scaffold 'Coaches' Immune Cells Into Scarless Spinal Cord Repair by Flipping Macrophages from Attack to Build"
pubDate: 2026-03-04
brief: "Usually, when the spinal cord heals, it builds a tough scar that acts like a wall, blocking nerves from growing back. This study uses new biomaterials to 'coach' the body's immune cells into a different type of healing — turning the injury site from a dead end into a bridge."
deepDive: "This study investigates a **biomimetic scaffold** composed of **hyaluronic acid and collagen**, functionalised with **anti-inflammatory IL-10**, to modulate the glial-immune interface post-SCI. In typical healing, **M1-polarised macrophages** and **reactive A1 astrocytes** promote a fibrotic environment leading to a dense **chondroitin sulfate proteoglycan (CSPG) scar**. This bioengineered approach facilitates a **phenotypic switch** in macrophages from the pro-inflammatory **M1** state to the pro-regenerative **M2** state. The shift significantly reduces deposition of **Type IV collagen and laminin** within the lesion. By 'orchestrating' this immune transition, the scaffold allows for regeneration of **GAP-43 positive axons** across the lesion epicentre — without encountering the mechanical or chemical barriers of the traditional glial scar. The result is a 'scarless' repair environment that converts the injury site from a permanent roadblock into a growth-permissive bridge."
