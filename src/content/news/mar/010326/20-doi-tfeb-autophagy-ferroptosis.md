title: "New Compound 20-DOI Activates the Cell's 'Master Cleaner' to Prevent Secondary SCI Damage"
pubDate: 2026-03-01
brief: "A new compound called 20-DOI has been shown to protect the spinal cord by activating a 'master cleaner' protein inside cells that clears out toxic waste before it can cause permanent paralysis."
deepDive: "Following traumatic injury, cells become cluttered with toxic waste and damaged components, which eventually kills them. **20-Deoxyingenol (20-DOI)** works by activating **TFEB** — the 'master regulator' of the cell's waste-disposal system known as **Autophagy**. When TFEB is switched on, the cell begins a 'deep clean,' breaking down the dangerous byproducts that lead to **Ferroptosis** (the iron-driven cell death also targeted in the nano-patch study). By keeping cells clean and operational, 20-DOI prevents the secondary 'wave' of damage that typically occurs in the days following an injury, preserving more healthy tissue and functional movement. The compound represents a promising early-stage pharmacological intervention for acute SCI."
