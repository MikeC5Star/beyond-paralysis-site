pubDate: 2026-02-27
brief: "A systematic review protocol sets out to rigorously evaluate whether deploying robotic exoskeletons during the very earliest stages of inpatient rehabilitation — rather than months later — helps people with incomplete spinal cord injuries regain natural walking ability faster than conventional therapy alone. The findings could make early exoskeleton training a standard clinical requirement."
