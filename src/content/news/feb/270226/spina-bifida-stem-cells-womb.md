title: "World-First: Placenta Stem Cells Applied to Spinal Cord During Womb Surgery"
pubDate: 2026-02-27
brief: "In a world-first clinical trial, six pregnant women whose fetuses had myelomeningocele — a severe form of spina bifida — received placenta-derived stem cells applied directly to the exposed spinal cord during in-utero surgery. All infants were born safely with intact repair sites and no spinal fluid leaks, demonstrating this pioneering approach is safe and significantly improves functional outcomes."
